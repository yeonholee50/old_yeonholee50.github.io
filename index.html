Intro

Hi, my name is Yeon, and I am a current undergraduate and research student at the Georgia Institute of Technology. Currently, I am majoring in computer science with threads in computer architecture and intelligence. I've always wanted to create a personal website where I can not only showcase my projects, but also discuss my interests outside of school Thankfully Github Pages is free, simple, and flexible, so I was able to create this website. Here is a link to some of my overall github repository.

I have built many projects through my college career. While projects I worked in class cannot be displayed on a public repository to protect the academic integrity of the institution and NDA for a capstone project, I will share my personal projects that I hope you will find interesting. Here is a link to my projects.

Outside of my career, I have a lot of interests. I love to cook and learn about different cuisines. It's through both food and music where I learn about other cultures. I'm also an avid traveler having been in 40 different countries and counting. In my spare time, I play as a midfielder for my school's intramural soccer team and chess (just below 1500 ELO).

It's actually through these two where my passion for computer science started. I'm part of the RoboJackets team that builds and deploys robots to play soccer on a 11 v 11 miniature field. These robots communicate with each other using proximity and location sensors and utilize non-traditional formations like 6-3-2 as opposed to a more traditional 4-3-3 to play a defensive structure that counter attacks with efficiency when the opposition overcommits at a certain area of the field.

My interest in chess is what led me to pick artificial intelligence as one my threads for my major as well as my current area of research. Starting off with projects where I used techniques like alpha-beta pruning to be able to search deeper in less time for a chess bot to my current research project where I help train a CNN to be able to recognize human hand motion to interpret sign language through supervised learning, my interest in AI has always been persistent. AI is here to stay and there are ethical boundaries yet to be discussed. However, I believe that if AI is used ethically and with appropriate measure, then it can be used to help progress the development of our society and economy. With the field of AI, the next biggest field will indefinitely be in data collection and labeling. This is especially true with the rise of self-driving cars and my current research area of computer vision with CNNs (Convolutional Neural Networks)where the bigger and less bias the dataset, the more "well-fitted" the CNN will be in classifying its inputs. Current datasets may seem big from a human perspective, but there is always going to be an inherent bias to the dataset as opposed to realtime input during testing. To prevent this, we need a large enough dataset (which we will make variations using random permutations). Even with large enough dataset, we will constantly need to be train our model to adhere to current trends.

About

This personal website serves as a testament to that pursuit, showcasing a dynamic collection of projects that reflect my dedication to excellence and innovation. From the code posted on GitHub to the unreleased projects that I've work on both in the classroom and from my previous internship, each endeavor is a step towards creating something impactful and extraordinary.
